# Outline

## Mode

- **Depth:** Standard

## Structure Verdict

Usable. The memo should stay short and keep every numerical claim attached to baseline, sample, and timeframe.

## Reader Journey

- In: The sponsor needs to know whether the pilot is worth another quarter.
- Out: The sponsor sees the pilot signal, the limit of the evidence, and the next decision.

## Section Architecture

| Section | Objective | Reader State In | Reader State Out | Main Claim | Evidence Hooks | Evidence Strength | Reader Questions | Objection Handled | Approx Length | Transition To Next | Keep/Cut |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|------------------|------------|----------------|-------------------|------------------|-------------------|---------------|--------------------|----------|
| 1. Decision | Put recommendation first | What should I approve? | Approve one more controlled quarter | Continue pilot, not broad rollout | C1, C2, C3 | Strong pilot metrics, bounded conclusion | Why continue? | One quarter is not proof | 120 words | Metric evidence | Keep |
| 2. Metric Evidence | Show the numbers with context | Are the numbers real enough? | Metrics are specific and bounded | Time and rework improved in pilot | C1, C2 | Strong with context | What baseline and sample? | Sample is small | 180 words | What not to claim | Keep |
| 3. Boundary | Prevent overclaiming | Can we claim ROI? | No, not yet | Evidence does not prove ROI or compliance | C3 | Moderate | What is excluded? | Avoids unsupported expansion | 120 words | Next quarter | Keep |
| 4. Next Quarter | Convert evidence into action | What happens next? | Sponsor knows next proof points | Continue with named review categories | C4 | Moderate | What will be tested? | Repeatability risk | 150 words | End | Keep |

## Objection Map

| Objection | Where Addressed | Handling |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| One quarter is too little proof | Boundary | Agree and limit claim to continuing the pilot |
| The sample may be too narrow | Metric Evidence | Name 20 sampled review packets and require broader next-quarter mix |
| Metrics may not translate to ROI | Boundary | Explicitly exclude ROI claims |

## Drafting Risks

- Do not say "proves."
- Do not separate percentages from baseline and denominator.
- Do not imply external benchmark performance.
