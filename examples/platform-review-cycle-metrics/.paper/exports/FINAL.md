# Platform Review Cycle Metrics

## Decision

Continue the review-cycle pilot for one more quarter. The synthetic metric pack shows enough operating signal to justify another controlled test, but not enough evidence to support a broad rollout or enterprise ROI claim.

The decision is therefore narrow: keep the pilot active, expand the packet mix, and require the next quarter to prove repeatability before the recommendation becomes larger.

## Metric Evidence

In the synthetic one-quarter pilot, median review cycle time moved from 10 days to 7 days, a 30% reduction across 20 sampled review packets (S1, S2).

Average rework items also moved from 6 to 4 per packet, a 33% reduction across the same 20 sampled review packets over one quarter (S1, S3).

Those are useful operating signals because they point in the same direction: shorter review time and fewer returns for rework. They are still pilot metrics. They should stay attached to the sample size and timeframe whenever they are repeated.

## Boundary

The pilot does not prove enterprise ROI, compliance improvement, or a general benchmark for review performance. S4 explicitly cautions against turning a one-quarter sample into broad financial or control claims.

That boundary matters. The evidence supports continuing the pilot; it does not support selling the pilot as a finished operating model.

## Next Quarter

Continue the pilot for payment exception triage, access reviews, and model-change packets. Those categories are concrete enough to test repeatability without turning the next quarter into a broad rollout.

The next proof point should be simple: if the broader packet mix preserves cycle-time and rework improvement while keeping review quality acceptable, the sponsor can consider a larger adoption decision. If the metrics weaken, the pilot should be narrowed or stopped.
