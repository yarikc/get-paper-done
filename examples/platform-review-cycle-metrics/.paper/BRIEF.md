# Brief

## Thesis

The review-cycle pilot should continue for one more quarter because the synthetic metric pack shows faster review completion and fewer rework items within the pilot scope, while the evidence is not strong enough to support enterprise-wide ROI claims.

## Claims

### Claim 1: Review cycle time improved in the pilot

- **What evidence supports it:** S1 and S2 report review cycle time moving from 10 days to 7 days across 20 sampled review packets over one quarter.
- **What could weaken it:** S4 warns that small pilots may overstate repeatability.

### Claim 2: Rework items decreased in the pilot

- **What evidence supports it:** S1 and S3 report average rework items moving from 6 to 4 per packet across the same 20 sampled review packets.
- **What could weaken it:** The sample excludes complex exception-heavy reviews.

### Claim 3: The pilot does not prove enterprise ROI

- **What evidence supports it:** S4 says the sample is too narrow for financial extrapolation.
- **What could weaken it:** A future finance-backed benefits analysis could support a broader claim.

## Reader Promise

The reader will understand what changed, why the recommendation is bounded, and what proof is needed before expanding the claim.

## Definition Of Done

- Numeric claims cite source IDs.
- Baseline, endpoint, sample, and timeframe appear with the numbers.
- Unsupported ROI and compliance claims are excluded.
