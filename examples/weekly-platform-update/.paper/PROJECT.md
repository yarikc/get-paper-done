# Weekly Platform Update Example

Keep this file short. `PROJECT.md` captures project identity and operating constraints; `BRIEF.md` owns the detailed thesis, claims, objections, and definition of done.

## What This Is

This is a short internal update for a platform delivery audience. It communicates current status, immediate blockers, next actions, and the specific help needed this week.

## Intended Outcome

The reader should understand what changed, what is blocked, what decision or help is needed, and what will happen before the next update.

## Format

- **Type:** Internal update
- **Target length:** 150-250 words
- **Publishing context:** Internal team / sponsor update
- **Deadline:** Weekly cadence

## Stakes

The stakes are coordination and follow-through, not publication or external persuasion. Weakness would cause confusion, missed dependencies, or duplicated follow-up.

## Source Policy

- **Citation style:** None
- **Evidence standard:** Team-operating evidence and current delivery state
- **Allowed sources:** Project status, team notes, and owner updates
- **Source storage:** No canonical research source set required for this low-risk update

## Boundaries

- Does not make regulatory, financial, market, or external claims.
- Does not decide roadmap strategy.
- Does not name real teams, people, organizations, or systems.

## Key Decisions

| Decision | Rationale | Status |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Use lite workflow | The paper is a low-risk internal status update. | Active |
| Skip canonical research | The update is based on current delivery state, not external evidence. | Active |
| Skip fact-check artifact | The draft makes no source-sensitive claims. | Active |
