# Responsible AI Controls Example

Keep this file short. `PROJECT.md` captures project identity and operating constraints; `BRIEF.md` owns the detailed thesis, claims, objections, and definition of done.

## What This Is

This is an external explainer for senior technology, data, risk, and product readers. It argues that responsible AI programs should prove operational control behavior, not only publish principles.

## Intended Outcome

The reader should understand why public AI governance claims need evidence from real operating controls, and what a practical control-evidence stack should include.

## Format

- **Type:** External explainer
- **Target length:** 900-1200 words
- **Publishing context:** Public technical article or external thought piece
- **Deadline:** Example fixture

## Stakes

The stakes are external credibility, customer trust, and public interpretation. Weakness would create overclaiming, imply guarantees the evidence does not support, or make governance sound like branding rather than operating discipline.

## Source Policy

- **Citation style:** Source IDs in draft artifacts
- **Evidence standard:** Synthetic source pack representing official, academic, industry, analyst, and news perspectives
- **Allowed sources:** Publicly citeable source classes only; no private client or employer material
- **Source storage:** Canonical source set lives in `.paper/RESEARCH.json`

## Boundaries

- Does not provide legal, regulatory, or compliance advice.
- Does not claim that any control framework guarantees safe AI outcomes.
- Does not name real organizations, products, customers, people, or systems.
- Uses synthetic source records so the fixture can be committed safely.
