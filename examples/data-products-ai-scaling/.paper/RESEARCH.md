# Research Index

## Summary

Research supports the bounded thesis: domain-aligned data products can help enterprise AI scaling when paired with common platform services, governance controls, ownership, quality expectations, and consumption contracts. The strongest evidence supports the mechanism, not a universal causal claim that data products directly improve AI business outcomes.

## Strongest Support

- Official cloud architecture guidance from Microsoft, Google Cloud, and AWS supports domain ownership, data product thinking, shared platform capabilities, and federated governance.
- NIST and ISO sources support the claim that AI systems depend on data quality, governance, risk management, and monitoring disciplines.
- The CxO argument is strongest when framed as reducing recurring AI delivery friction, not as guaranteeing AI outcomes.

## Main Counterargument

Domain-aligned data products can become fragmented local silos if governance, metadata, access controls, semantics, quality monitoring, incentives, and platform responsibilities are weak.

## Claims To Soften

- Replace "domain-aligned data products enable AI scaling" with "domain-aligned data products can reduce recurring data friction in AI delivery when paired with shared platform controls and clear ownership."
- Replace "the common platform prevents fragmentation" with "a common governed platform is the control layer that can keep domain alignment from becoming fragmentation."

## Open Gaps

- Public case studies directly linking data products to AI delivery outcomes.
- Regulated financial-services-specific control requirements.
- Concrete practitioner postmortems where data-product programs failed or increased fragmentation.
- Direct public case studies linking domain-aligned data products to measured AI delivery improvement.
