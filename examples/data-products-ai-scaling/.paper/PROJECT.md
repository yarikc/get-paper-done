# Consolidating Enterprise Data Platforms Around Domain-Aligned Data Products to Enable AI Scaling

## What This Is

An executive strategy paper arguing that enterprise AI scaling depends less on adding model tooling and more on organizing trusted, governed, domain-aligned data products on top of a common platform foundation. This workspace is a completed reference example for the GPD workflow.

## Intended Outcome

Readers should understand why domain-aligned data products can make AI delivery more scalable, where the model can fail, and what governance and platform decisions must be made before expanding the approach.

## Format

- **Type:** Strategy paper
- **Target length:** 2,500 to 3,500 words
- **Publishing context:** Internal executive and architecture audience; possible later adaptation into public technical thought leadership
- **Deadline:** None for publication; this is a reusable example workspace

## Stakes

Enterprise AI programs often stall because data ownership, quality, lineage, controls, and consumption patterns are not designed for repeated reuse. The topic matters now because organizations are investing heavily in AI while still operating data platforms that were optimized for centralized reporting or local application needs.

## Source Policy

- **Citation style:** Inline links or source IDs in research artifacts
- **Evidence standard:** Practitioner evidence, official documentation, analyst/industry context, and credible technical sources
- **Allowed sources:** Web research allowed; user-provided material may be added later
- **Source storage:** Paper-specific reference material belongs in `.paper/sources/`

## Boundaries

- This paper will not recommend a specific vendor platform.
- It will not claim that domain-aligned data products automatically produce AI outcomes.
- It will not provide legal or regulatory advice.
- It will avoid confidential internal claims and mark unsupported organizational assertions for research or qualification.
