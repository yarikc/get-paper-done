# Audience

## Audience Selection

- **Primary audience:** CxO reader
- **Co-primary technical audience:** Distinguished architect / engineer
- **Audience shape:** Prioritized multi-audience
- **Priority rule:** The paper must first be decision-useful for executives, but every major claim must survive technical scrutiny from senior architects.

## CxO Reader

### Current Beliefs

- AI scaling is strategically urgent, but many platform papers overfocus on mechanisms.
- Data-platform investment competes with many other priorities.
- Strategy without execution logic is not actionable.

### Needs

- Clear investment logic.
- Why now.
- What changes if the argument is accepted.
- Execution risks and governance implications.
- A practical next move, not a broad transformation slogan.

### Likely Objections

- "This sounds like another data platform modernization pitch."
- "What business decision does this enable?"
- "Why will domain alignment reduce friction rather than create more local variation?"
- "Where is the proof that this helps AI outcomes?"

## Distinguished Architect / Engineer

### Current Beliefs

- Executive technology papers often simplify the hard parts.
- Data products are useful only if ownership, interfaces, quality, semantics, lineage, and consumption contracts are concrete.
- AI scaling claims often outrun evidence.

### Needs

- Clear mechanism.
- Boundaries and failure modes.
- Alternative patterns and trade-offs.
- Honest distinction between platform foundation, domain ownership, and AI consumption.

### Likely Objections

- "Domain-aligned data products can become inconsistent local silos."
- "A common platform does not guarantee common semantics."
- "The hard part is operating model and incentives, not architecture diagrams."
- "AI use cases may need feature, governance, and monitoring patterns not covered by generic data products."

## Desired Reader Shift

After reading, the executive reader should believe that domain-aligned data products deserve focused investment as an AI scaling prerequisite, but only if paired with common platform services, governance controls, and explicit consumption contracts.

After reading, the architect reader should believe the argument is technically bounded, mechanism-based, and honest about alternatives.

## Proof Standard

- Named sources and explicit research confidence.
- Evidence for and against the thesis.
- Clear distinction between factual claims, strategic judgments, technical mechanisms, and recommendations.
- Regulatory and control implications qualified rather than asserted.

## Scoring Emphasis

- Decision usefulness
- Mechanism quality
- Evidence sufficiency
- Objection handling
- Trade-off clarity
- Boundary clarity
- Jargon appropriateness

## Conflict Rule

When executive clarity conflicts with technical precision, the main text should stay executive-readable and the technical mechanism should be captured in concise sections, tables, or callouts. Do not hide core technical assumptions in appendices if they materially affect the recommendation.
