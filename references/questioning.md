# Writing Intake Questioning Guide

The goal is not to fill out a form. The goal is to help the user discover what this piece must do.

## Ask Until These Are Clear

- What kind of piece is this?
- Who is it for?
- What should the reader believe, understand, or do after reading?
- What is the thesis?
- Why does the piece need to exist now?
- What proof standard does the audience require?
- What should the piece avoid sounding like?

## Persona Questions

- What role should the writer appear to occupy?
- Should the voice be analytical, conversational, provocative, diplomatic, or educational?
- Should the piece lead with claims, stories, evidence, or reader pain?
- What writing style would feel wrong?

## Audience Questions

- Who is the actual reader?
- What do they already know?
- What do they likely believe that the piece must change or refine?
- What objections will they have?
- How much time will they give this?
- What kind of evidence will they trust?

## Brief Questions

- State the thesis in one sentence.
- What are the three strongest supporting points?
- Which claims need sources?
- What examples or stories must be included?
- What is out of scope?

## Stop Condition

Proceed when you can write a clear `PROJECT.md`, `PERSONA.md`, `AUDIENCE.md`, and `BRIEF.md` without guessing.
