# Public Technical Reader

Use this curated audience persona for public technical writing: blogs, LinkedIn posts, newsletters, and external essays.

## Audience Boundary

This profile describes the reader model, objections, proof standard, desired reader shift, and reusable adaptation rules. It does not define interactive TUI behavior, file snapshot policy, feedback approval mechanics, or workflow gates.

## Reader

- **Primary audience:** Technical practitioner, engineering leader, data/AI/platform professional, or informed public technical reader.
- **Role/context:** Reads for insight, novelty, practical framing, and language they can reuse.
- **Expertise level:** Informed practitioner, but mixed depth across subdomains.
- **Time/patience:** Low to medium. Tolerates depth only when payoff is clear.

## Current Beliefs

They likely believe:

- Most public tech writing repeats familiar takes.
- AI and platform topics are overloaded with hype.
- Practical insights are more valuable than polished abstractions.

## Needs

They need:

- Insight: a useful frame they did not already have.
- Novelty: a non-obvious claim, example, or mechanism.
- Practicality: implications they can apply or debate.
- Low fluff: tight prose, concrete examples, minimal throat-clearing.

## Objections

They may object that:

- The draft says something familiar in polished language.
- The argument is too enterprise-internal or too generic.
- The paper uses jargon without payoff.
- The opening takes too long to reach the point.

## Desired Shift

After reading, they should:

- **Understand:** A sharper frame or mechanism.
- **Believe:** The author has practical, non-generic insight.
- **Do:** Rethink, share, save, comment, or apply the idea.

## Adaptation Rules

- **Context depth:** Low to medium. Start fast; explain only what unlocks the insight.
- **Technical depth:** Medium. Keep technical credibility without overloading.
- **Proof standard:** Concrete examples, clear mechanism, credible lived reality, and useful implications.
- **Tone fit:** Direct, practical, original, low fluff, no hype.

## Draft Scoring Emphasis

- Insight novelty
- Practical usefulness
- Opening strength
- Low fluff tolerance
- Jargon appropriateness
