# Business Or Operating Executive

Use this curated audience persona for business leaders and operating executives who need practical implications.

## Reader

- **Primary audience:** Business executive, operating executive, transformation leader, or functional leader accountable for outcomes.
- **Role/context:** Owns cost, delivery, adoption, risk, people, process, and timing.
- **Expertise level:** Informed non-expert to executive.
- **Time/patience:** Will engage if the paper connects technology to outcomes and operating implications quickly.

## Current Beliefs

They likely believe:

- Technology recommendations often hide cost and organizational burden.
- Outcomes matter more than architecture purity.
- Timing, ownership, and adoption risk determine whether ideas work.

## Needs

They need:

- Outcomes: what improves and for whom.
- Costs: investment, complexity, organizational load, and opportunity cost.
- Organizational implications: ownership, operating model, skills, governance, and adoption.
- Timing: what happens now, later, and what must be sequenced.

## Objections

They may object that:

- The paper is too technical or abstract.
- The operating impact is vague.
- Costs and adoption risks are understated.
- The recommendation lacks timing or ownership clarity.

## Desired Shift

After reading, they should:

- **Understand:** What business or operating problem the recommendation solves.
- **Believe:** The proposal is practical and worth operational attention.
- **Do:** Align resources, ask sharper questions, sponsor the work, or adjust priorities.

## Adaptation Rules

- **Context depth:** Medium. Translate technical claims into operating implications.
- **Technical depth:** Medium-low unless needed for trust.
- **Proof standard:** Outcome logic, cost/risk clarity, practical examples, operating model implications.
- **Tone fit:** Practical, direct, operational, no jargon wall.

## Draft Scoring Emphasis

- Outcome clarity
- Cost/risk clarity
- Organizational implications
- Timing and sequencing
- Decision usefulness
